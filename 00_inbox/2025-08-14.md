Threat modelling?

Threat modeling has several **common models/frameworks** that help you systematically identify, analyze, and mitigate security risks.  
Here’s a rundown of the **main threat modeling models** you’ll encounter:

---

## **1. STRIDE** (Microsoft)

- **Goal:** Identify threats based on categories of possible attacks.
    
- **Acronym stands for:**
    - **S**poofing identity
    - **T**ampering with data
    - **R**epudiation (denying actions)
    - **I**nformation disclosure
    - **D**enial of service
    - **E**levation of privilege        
- **Best for:** Application-level security, especially in early design phases.
---
## **2. DREAD** (Microsoft – now deprecated internally, but still used in some contexts)

- **Goal:** Rate the risk level of threats.
- **Stands for:**
    - **D**amage potential
    - **R**eproducibility
    - **E**xploitability
    - **A**ffected users
    - **D**iscoverability
- **Best for:** Prioritizing remediation efforts.
---
## **3. PASTA** (Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis)

- **Goal:** A 7-step risk-centric methodology focusing on simulating attacks to determine business impact.    
- **Steps:** Define objectives, decompose application, identify threats, analyze vulnerabilities, model attacks, analyze risks, define countermeasures. 
- **Best for:** Organizations that want to align security with business impact.
---
## **4. Trike**
- **Goal:** Risk management-based threat modeling focusing on defining a security requirements model and translating it into an implementation model.
- **Best for:** Projects that need strong traceability between security requirements and testing.
---
## **5. VAST** (Visual, Agile, and Simple Threat)
- **Goal:** Integrate threat modeling into agile/DevOps pipelines at scale. 
- **Best for:** Large enterprises with distributed development teams; tool-friendly.
---
## **6. OCTAVE** (Operationally Critical Threat, Asset, and Vulnerability Evaluation)

- **Goal:** Identify and prioritize risks based on organizational needs rather than only technical weaknesses.
- **Best for:** High-level, organization-wide risk assessments.    

---

## **7. Attack Trees**
- **Goal:** Represent attacks as a tree structure where the root is the main goal and branches represent different ways to achieve it.
- **Best for:** Visualizing possible attack paths for a specific target.

---

✅ **Summary Table:**

| Model        | Focus Area                        | Best For                   |
| ------------ | --------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| STRIDE       | Threat categories                 | App-level design phase     |
| DREAD        | Risk rating                       | Prioritizing fixes         |
| PASTA        | Attack simulation & business risk | Risk-driven orgs           |
| Trike        | Requirements to testing trace     | Compliance-heavy projects  |
| VAST         | Agile/DevOps integration          | Large-scale orgs           |
| OCTAVE       | Organizational risk               | Business-level assessments |
| Attack Trees | Visual attack paths               | Specific threat scenarios  |
